Week 1 - Value

  • Notan-dominant or chiaroscurodominant?
  • Exposed for light or shadow?
    • Expose for LIGHT for
      • Sculptural modeling of a form
      • Darker overall image due to dark flat shadows
    • Expose for SHADOW for:
      • Brilliantly colored shadows, reflected light
      • Brighter overall image due to blown-out lights
  • High, medium, or low major key? (proportion of light to dark, mostly light = high, mostly dark = low)
  • High medium, or low minor key? (difference in contrast between lightest and darkest value)
  • Control value range (compress or decompress) with adjustment layers
    • Darken Layer: Darkens values higher than the colour of the layer
    • Lighten Layer: Lightens values lower than the colour of the layer

Week 2 - Hue

Process

  1. Making sheets of objects in flat in a seperate file in greyscale
  2. Duplicate group of object in flat to distort
  3. Lasso out rest of shapes in greyscale
  4. Wtih Auto-Select on move tool, Ctrl+U Hue/Saturation slider with Colorize checked to set colour to the local colour
  5. Analyze lighting and value and start putting adjustment layers to paint in shadow and light, emphasize and exaggerate shadow shapes
  6. For lights, duplicate layer and use a motion blur, think about warm/cool contrast
  7. Curves - using individual channels to adjust the temperature contrast of the light and shadows. Pull up or left to increase influence of channel, down or right to increase influence of the opposite. For example, red up or left to increase red influence or down or right to increase influence of cyan. Selective Color - adjust temperature with individual colour groups
