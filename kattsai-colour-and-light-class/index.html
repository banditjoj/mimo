Week 1 - Value

  • Notan-dominant or chiaroscurodominant?
  • Exposed for light or shadow?
    • Expose for LIGHT for
      • Sculptural modeling of a form
      • Darker overall image due to dark flat shadows
    • Expose for SHADOW for:
      • Brilliantly colored shadows, reflected light
      • Brighter overall image due to blown-out lights
  • High, medium, or low major key? (proportion of light to dark, mostly light = high, mostly dark = low)
  • High medium, or low minor key? (difference in contrast between lightest and darkest value)
  • Control value range (compress or decompress) with adjustment layers
    • Darken Layer: Darkens values higher than the colour of the layer
    • Lighten Layer: Lightens values lower than the colour of the layer

Week 2 - Hue

Process

  1. Making sheets of objects in flat in a seperate file in greyscale

  2. Duplicate group of object in flat to distort

  3. Lasso out rest of shapes in greyscale

  4. Wtih Auto-Select on move tool, Ctrl+U Hue/Saturation slider with Colorize checked to set colour to the local colour

  5. Analyze lighting and value and start putting adjustment layers to paint in shadow and light, emphasize and exaggerate shadow shapes

  6. For lights, duplicate layer and use a motion blur, think about warm/cool contrast

  7. Curves - using individual channels to adjust the temperature contrast of the light and shadows. Pull up or left to increase influence of channel, down or right to increase influence of the opposite. For example, red up or left to increase red influence or down or right to increase influence of cyan. Selective Color - adjust temperature with individual colour groups

    Week 3 - Light & Atmosphere

    Shadows shortest at noon and longest at sundown and sunrise

4 Features to Consider

  • Temperature of light and shadow
  • Strength, size, and direction of light
  • Reflected light
  • Major and Minor keys

Major key increases rightward, minor key increases downward

  • Strong contrast and crisp shapes between different building sides and shadows
  • Shadows lighter due to sky bounce light

  • Objects gradually darker downward
  • Paint forms in local colour to design Notan value matrix

  • Sunset banding: just a portion of an object is illuminated with sunlight

  • Doesn't need to be saturated with blue: creating the impression of blue light

  • Light from sun bouncing off of the moon

Taller trees produce larger and softer dapple, shorter trees produce smaller and harder dapple

Process:

  1. Tinting base colours with adjustment layer or Color Lookup
  2. Establish broad hard shadows first and then softer shadows with Multiply adjustment layer
  3. Determine brightest source(s) and temperature of light
  4. Hard Light (right) - groups local colours and values closer together than Overlay
  5. (left)
  6. Place basic lights, then influence of lights on surroundings
  7. Remember temperature contrast between light and shadow, brown shadow + blue light vs blue shadow + blue light
  8. Where is the light hitting the walls, floors and object in the references and how to stylize it?
  9. Pinterest reference: room + type of lighting or time of day
  10. Cyan reaches max saturation at a very high value
  11. Emphasize and exaggerate temperature changes
  12. Make shapes majority shadow or majority light
  13. Keep airbrushed lights small to avoid haziness

Week 4 - Stylized Colour

Watercolour brush with foreground/background colour toggle with cool and warm swap to get variation in shadow temperature

Process:

  1. Lay in flat neutral colours
  2. Shift values for nighttime
  3. Apply gradient map (adjustment mode lighten and darken for lower effect)
  4. Adjust values underneath gradient map
  5. Add lights and atmosphere in to have variation aftter gradient map
  6. Final colour adjustments + add accent colour if desired

Haziness = light affecting light and shadow in same ratio, seperate values more

Connected Shadow = only if object is directly touching the wall/floor

Consider outside light from window reflecting onto floor and ceiling

Golden Hour = blue sky at top, hard saturated light on objects & sun is right above the horizon line so window casts will be almost horizontal
