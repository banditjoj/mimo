Gabriel Gomez: Speed Painting in Photoshop

Artist Influences

  • Van Gogh

  • Eyvind Earle

  • Hiroshi Yoshida

  • Marcos Mateu-Mestre

  • He creates brushes for each painting

    • 3 different leaves of same species to avoid repetition in big, medium, small

  • Dry / Cold Environments - thin small leaves, Jungle - big wide leaves
  • Actions: Back Shadow + Rim Light, Glow
  • Layer Settings - Shape Transparency, affects how certain Layer Modes like Color Doge Work

Atmospheric Perspective

  • Sky - Back Blue, Pollution - Grey Blur, Emphasize with Saturated Foreground and Greyer Back

  • Contrast in FG, Values close to sky in BG
  • Atmosphere - Normal, Lights - Lighten, Shadows - Overlay

References

  • Pick a specific place and gather 3 images from there, can do the same with another location and combine references from 2 different places

  • Choose reference with specificity, e.g. Jungle in Central America

  • Look at references and see what makes them look good and specific? e.g. water + caustic effects, beautiful roots, vegetation coming from top, specific plant species, specific contrast of textures, specific ecosystem

  • Sketch - line work, value, or colour, using parts from reference to put into composition + general camera angle, think about shape language

  • Flat Colour Block - Use Local Colour, Use Layers to separate FG / MG / BG, block everything out first and adjust colours later,

  • ALT + SHIFT = select cross section between selection and created

  • Texture + Pattern - add detail without adding too much contrast or distracting from overall composition, variety of patterns if one is horizontal, do the other vertical, impression of different tree species
  • Paint Texture flat, then map using transform to create scale difference + depth

  • Avoid straight down lighting, think about lighting conditions and shadow shapes, draw 3D arrow to indicate direction of lighting
  • Identify primary (sun) and secondary (bounce light, ambient) light sources, think about shadow colours and ambient occlusion
  • Shadow - Create Fill Shadow for entire canvas and copy it to all layers, use alpha mask to paint in light on shadow layer

  • Remember big medium small for light shapes!

  • Avoid equal parts of light and shadow, choose one to dominate

  • Bounce Light - Screen

  • Subsurface - Color Dodge (Linear)

  • Prefer lower fill to opacity with light layers

  • Smudge - edges of cloud

  • Applying Linear Light to inverse of shadow layer

  • Atmosphere - use to push values closer together: Lighten layer

  • Light Rays - magical look

  • Lens Blur + Color Adjustments for final

  • Making variations â†’ skew + colour adjust + layer modes to make new compositions to reuse for future paintings or new colour ideas
